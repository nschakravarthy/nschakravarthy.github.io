Melbourne / Product manager · Learning in public

Building AI foundations, from first principles.

I've been in product and engineering long enough to know a real shift when I see one, and AI is it. What excites me is that it's raising the bar for product craft in ways we haven't seen before. Every serious decision in an AI product — reliability, trust, latency, cost — eventually touches something technical enough that you need real intuition to get it right. Not just API-level familiarity, but a feel for how things actually work under the hood.

That's what I'm building. I work through things from scratch — autograd engines, transformers, vector databases, inference servers — and write about what I figure out along the way. I believe product instincts get sharper when they're grounded in how the technology actually works.

The bigger picture: I think the most exciting AI applications over the next decade will be in physical and vertical industries — manufacturing, robotics, medical devices, infrastructure. Places where the problems are harder and the stakes are higher. I want to be useful in those rooms, and you earn that seat by doing the foundational work first.

Get in touch if any of this resonates.
